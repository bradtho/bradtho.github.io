>I've been actively procrastinating about writing a blog for the longest time. The need to start keeping all my technical notes somewhere that wasn't a piece of paper has become increasingly important. This blog is intended to be a many part saga with multiple pathways. Don't expect them all to be applicable to you, pick and choose the roads that make your journey most relative to your particular circumstances.
{: .notice--success}

## What is this whole journey is about

**...and why are you really spending all this time writing a blog?**

I discovered [this](https://roadmap.sh/devops) awesome flowchart when I first started at my current workplace. I felt it was a great introduction for someone who was first starting out in the world of DevOps. However, what if you're someone like me who's come from the world of ClickOps and Hardware Systems and Networking? I felt that it was populated with items that weren't entirely applicable to me such as the block on "Understand different OS concepts" and I think too that most Engineers with a few years under their belt will have this covered off.

So it's at this point the procrastination sets in... it's taken me nearly 2 years but here it is - the streamlined version of ClickOps to DevOps

![full](/assets/images/pathways.png)
{: .full}

There's no real reason to start with *"Learn a Programming Language"* but given I think it will be the most timely of tasks (especially if you have little to no programming experience) then it's a pretty decent start.

One thing I do challenge everyone to try is to *"Live in the Terminal"* for one month - yeah OK I haven't done this myself but I reckon it'll make an awesome blog post when I eventually do!

So hopefully this diagram will not only act as a bit of a site map for this blog but help guide other's wanting to get out of ClickOps and be a really useful Engine(er)

Thanks for reading!
