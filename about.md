## What this whole journey is about

**So why are you really spending all this time writing a blog?**

**OK, so what the hell is DevOps Engineer?** Well it's a bit of an in joke at my workplace that there is no such thing. The premise being that just because you are practitioner of DevOps does not make you an Engineer - how you'd *"engineer"* an organisational culture I'm unsure? Anyway, I am going down a controversial rabbit hole that I'd really rather stay out of. So, for future context, when I say "DevOps Engineer", I'm talking Site Reliability, Platform or Cloud Engineering as an actual job function even though your title may differ.

**So, hang-on what's the difference between a DevOps Engineer and a Systems Administrator?** Ummm… a lot and probably more than I could list here and rather than do that, it might better serve to reshape the question and ask why would you want to stop being a SysAdmin and jump into DevOps? I can't answer this for you but I can certainly tell you why I decided to make the switch…

**Why did I make the jump?** I did it all for the money! well… that's not entirely correct but I did see the writing was on the wall for Systems Administrator roles as they began to dwindle in favour job descriptions that talked of requiring Automation, Infrastructure as Code, Containerisation, Kubernetes, \<insert Cloud Provider Name here\>, Agile, Open Source, Cloud Native… and when I looked at my resume and saw 10+ years of skills in TCP/IP Networking*, Server Hardware, Data Centre, Windows OS, Exchange, Storage I knew that it was time to start doing what all the cool kids were doing and jump onto this DevOps bandwagon or face career extinction.

*I'll talk about why this is still a really important skill even in DevOps roles in a future article.

OK so maybe I am starting to talk about the difference between Systems Administration and DevOps Engineering, so rather than do that I highly recommend reading Site Reliability Engineering and its sister book The Site Reliability Workbook - they're free to read online but are easy enough to obtain in soft or hard copy.
