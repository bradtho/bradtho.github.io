## The New (Azure) Journey

So I've definitely not sold my soul (entirely) to Microsoft with this change - I didn't have one to start with :smiling_imp: but that's a different story - and as I explained in my ["A Big Change"](/posts/a-big-change) post I'll be focusing primarily on Azure Platform Services for the foreseeable future. It didn't make a huge amount of sense to me to keep writing topics about technology that I won't actually implementing.

You'll definitely continue to see some Open Source projects, heck even a number of the Microsoft ones are Open Source!. However, the bulk of my writings will be about Platform Engineering in the Azure eco-system. This blog will continue to be about being Cloud Native and about how to move away from the "old-world" so I will not be writing about the Windows Operating System. You'll definitely be seeing a lot of Linux, Config and Code Snippets.

>I will not be writing about the Windows Operating System.

![full](/assets/images/azure_pathways.png)
{: .full}

Aside from being easier to read :trollface: there's really only one major addition I'd like to discuss in the new Journey and that's in the "things to learn" category. I realised I'd missed a major learning step from the original - **security**. If you've not been living under a rock you'll know that Security skills are in huge demand. In general though, security is just a good thing to do everywhere and keep in front of mind. To that end I did throw in the **DevSecOps** pathway, but in my opinion if you're doing security right there should be no need to differentiate it as an actual function.

In fact my next post will likely be a post about one announcement or another from [Microsoft Build](https://bit.ly/3c34FgE) and being full of contradictions I imagine it'll probably have something to do with DevSecOps. :smile:
